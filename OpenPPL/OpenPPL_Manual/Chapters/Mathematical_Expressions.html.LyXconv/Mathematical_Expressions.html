Chapter 1
Mathematical Expressions

Of course OpenPPL supports arbitary complex mathematical expressions. As as example let us consider odds and outs.

// Calling according to odds and outs 
WHEN (AmountToCall / (AmountToCall + PotSize) > Outs / CardsLeft) CALL FORCE

Off course this example is a bit simplistic. It does not consider other players in the pot, implied odds on future streets, the chance to semi-bluff, etc. But I think you get the point.

The following operators are supported:

Logical Operators

The logical operators “and”, “or”, and “not” should be pretty self-explaining.

Operator

Example

And

WHEN (BotIsLastRaiser AND OpponentsLeft = 1 AND Bets = 0 And ... BETHALFPOT FORCE)

Or

WHEN (Hand = AA OR Hand = KK) RAISEMAX FORCE

Not

WHEN (HaveNothing AND OpponentsLeft >= 2 AND NOT BotIsLastRaiser) CHECK FORCE

AND has higher priority than OR and NOT has highest of all, so if you want to write an expression like

WHEN (AmountToCall <= 4 AND ((Hand = 22) OR (HAND = 33)...))

you have to throw in some extra brackets, otherwise the bot will call with 33 any bet and that is probably not what you want. More complicated expressions sometimes lead to confusions. If you have a problem with that you might want to revisit the basics of mathematical logic and the “Laws of De Morgan”.

Arithmetical Operators

OpenPPL also supports basic arithmetics. The usual rules apply of course. The percentage-operator has the same priority like multiplication and division, which is higher than addition, subtraction.
