Handstrength-library

OpenHoldem provides a function-library, developed by Winngy and HTC, that evaluates the strength of your hand and might be a good starting-point for a beginner. The library is available for download at x!!!. Basically, the f$mh_str symbols (e.g. f$mh_str_twopair) return a relative ranking of your hand (hole and common) from 1 to 5.

Symbol

Meaning

f$mh_3straightxy

(x=1 for wheel, 0 not, y=1 for broadway, 0 not) - returns true if the board has a wheel straight draw or broadway straight draw, given the wheel/broadway parameters 00 - true if the board has neither a wheel straight draw nor a broadway straight draw 10 - true if the board has a wheel straight draw 01 - true if the board has a broadway straight draw 11 - true if the board has either a wheel straight draw or a broadway straight draw

f$mh_bottomsd

true if I have a bottom straight draw (if you are contributing a single card to an open-ended straight draw and that card is the smallest, this symbol is true e.g. hole: T2 common: 345K)

f$mh_nsuitedbetter

number of missing suited cards that are higher than my best suited card

f$mh_kickerbetter

number of cards that can beat your kicker f$mh_kickerrank rank of your kicker (returns 0 if kicker is shared [board] and thus useless)

f$mh_nouts

number of outs (HTC’s formula) f$mh_str_strflush 0-5 (5 best) of the relative strength of your straight flush

f$mh_str_quads

0-5 (5 best) of the relative strength of your four of a kind

f$mh_str_fullhouse

0-5 (5 best) of the relative strength of your full house

f$mh_str_flush

0-5 (5 best) of the relative strength of your flush

f$mh_str_straight

0-5 (5 best) of the relative strength of your straight

f$mh_str_trips

0-5 (5 best) of the relative strength of your three of a kind

f$mh_str_twopair

0-5 (5 best) of the relative strength of your two pair

f$mh_str_onepair

0-5 (5 best) of the relative strength of your one pair
