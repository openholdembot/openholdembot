Chapter 1
Favourite Failures

1.1. OH does not connect to the table (I)

Reason: Table not recognized because of incorrect system-settings.

To recognize a table the following criteria have to match:

  • size of the window
  • table-title
  • maybe some tablepoints to distinguish similar maps (e.g. FL, NL-SNG or different skins from the same network).

Usually the problem is the window-size caused by incorrect system-settings; Windows XP and Vista posses a higher titlebar than Windows 2000 classic-theme.

1.2. OH does not connect to the table (II)

Reason: No tablemap loaded.

There are two ways to load a tablemap:

  • explicitely via the menu: Menu -> File -> Load Table Map
  • implicitely from the “scraper”-folder in the installation-directory of OpenHoldem.

1.3. OH does not play

Reason: You did not turn on the autoplayer.

Click the diamond-button to the right of the green and red circles in the toolbar.

1.4. The autoplayer-button can’t be enabled

Reason: Userchair not recognized.

The autoplayer can only be enabled, after OpenHoldem has recognized your chair. To do so OpenHoldem must see

  • your cards
  • some buttons, so OpenHoldem really knows, it is your turn and not a gamestate with cards shown for your opponents (i.e. a showdown).

1.5. OH calls any hand

Reason: Tablemap does not detect bet amounts.

If OpenHoldem calls every hand — except maybe the strong ones — this is probably neither a bug in OpenHoldem nor bad logic in the bot but a problem with the Tablemap or with your settings again: The TM does most probably not recognize your opponents bet, so OpenHoldem thinks, you have a free play and calls.

Whenever you experience strange actions, you should ask yourself the following questions (http://forum.winholdem.net/wbb/viewtopic.php?f=38&t=12408):

  • is the scraper reading the bet values correctly?
  • is the scraper reading my cardfaces correctly?
  • is the scraper reading opponent cardbacks correctly?
  • do the blind values look sane?
  • does the site provide handnumbers?
  • are my action buttons being seen when my turn arrives?

You can check the values for these symbols in the debug-tab of the formula-editor.

 = br 
 = sblind 
 = bblind 
 = handnumber 
 = nplayersdealt 
 = myturnbits

1.6. OH does not work at ManualMode

Reason: Not your turn or userchair not recognized.

To work properly OpenHoldem must have recognized the userchair. See !!! Also some symbols are only valid, when it is your turn to act. So you should highlight some buttons in ManualMode.

1.7. OH acts multiple times

Reason: Improper bring-delay and scraper-delay.

The bring-delay should be equal or even smaller than the scraper-delay. Otherwise it can happen, that OH acts, but bring doesn’t display a new image in time. So when the next heart-beat-cycle starts, OH will scrape the exact same game-state another time and act another time.

1.9. Perl (or the user-DLL) crashes immediatelly

Reason: Usage of functionality, that is not yet initialized.

The probably most prominent example is the usage of the function “getsym” during the initialization-phase of Perl or a DLL.

DOUBLE BIGBLIND = GETSYM(“BBLIND”);

BigBlind is here a global variable, that gets initialized immediatelly, when the DLL gets loaded. But the function getsym uses a callback-pointer, that gets initialized later with one of the first messages from OpenHoldem to the DLL. So you dereference an undefined pointer and crash because of a memory-access-error.

Very similar are debug messages into a file, that has not yet been opened for writing or an inexpertly load-order of Perl-modules, that do similar stuff as descibed above during the phase of initialization.
